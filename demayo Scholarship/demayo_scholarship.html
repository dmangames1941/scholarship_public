What is Driving Under the Influence?

Driving under the influence(or what I will here on out refer to as DUI) is the action of driving while you are under the influence by any sort of alcohol or drugs. DUI often impaires a drivers vision and ability to make quick decisions which is quinitessential when driving any sort of automobile or motorbike. If you get caught driving under the influence you will most of the time be jailed for a minimum of 24 hours to a rare maximum of 6 months in jails as well as be charged a fines ranging from $500 - $2000 and in some occasions be obligated to install an ignition interlock device which requires the driver to take an in car breathalizer each time before they start the car. It can also lead to the suspension of your drivers license.

Why You Should Not Drive Under the Influence

Driving under the influence is one of the most serious driving offenses next to speeding in a school zone and passing a school bus with red flashing lights on. Doing this not only puts you at risk of possibly getting killed, but in many situations it is another driver who is punished for your stupid descision. Why put you or anyone else at risk with you driving drunk when we have things like Uber and Lyft which allows you to get home safely.

Statitics and Information About DUI

According to the NCDOT Crash Facts report from 2019/2020:

  • There were 11,475 alcohol related crashes
  • 64.71% (7,426/11,475) alcohol related crashes resulted in injury
  • 3.59% (412/11,475) alcohol related crashes resulted in fatalities.
  • Wake, Mecklenburg, and Guilford counties placed in the top 3 for drunk driving car accidents, accidents with injury, and resulted in fatality(as of 2020).
  • Charlotte placed the highest in alcohol related crashes, injury, and fatalities followed by Raleigh, Wiston-Salem, Greensboro, and Durham
  • In 2020 the number of teen related drunk driving crashes increased to 1,014 from 975 in 2019.
  • Out of all of the holiday's celebrated in the US crashes caused by DUI is the highest during christmas, the most injuries during Labor Day, and the most fatalities on labor day although 4th of July is not too far behind.
  • Weirdly enough in 2019 the number of alcohol related trafic fatalities is down almost 56% since 1985.
